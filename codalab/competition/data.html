Data links

Data for the competition is available on our git repository. The complete datasets will be made available at the end of the evaluation phase (January 31st).

Data format & contents

All datasets are in JSON format. We minimize whitespace for memory consumption purposes. Trial, training and development datasets for both definition modeling and reverse dictionary tracks are shared: as the two tasks are converse tasks, the source for either one is the target of the other. Test datasets will be distinct, and have no overlap between the two tracks.

Datasets are available for the five following languages: EN, ES, FR, IT, RU. Datasets are distinct per languages. All five languages contain embeddings for two distinct types of architectures: "char" (character-based embeddings) and "sgns" (word2vec Skip-Gram with Negative Sampling). EN, FR and RU have in addition a third type of embedding available, namely "electra" (Transformer-based contextual embeddings).

All embeddings for any given language were trained on the same corpus; embedding training corpora across languages were designed to be comparable. All computation details will be provided in the task description paper.

The trial dataset is annotated much more richly than the other datasets. During the shared task, we will only provide gloss, id and embeddings. After the evaluation phase, we will provide complete datasets, which will also include the word being defined and its part of speech.

We very strongly encourage participants to reserve the trial dataset for running manual evaluations of their systems' production. The presence of a manual evaluation in system descriptions will be taken into account during the reviewing process and discussed in the task paper.
